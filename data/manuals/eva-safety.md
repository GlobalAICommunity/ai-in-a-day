# Orbital Operations Manual — EVA Safety

*Document EV-300 · Revision 2 · Classification: Crew Essential*

## 1. Overview

An EVA (Extra-Vehicular Activity) is any crewed excursion outside the pressurised
modules onto the Martian surface. EVAs carry the highest personal risk of any routine
colony activity. **No EVA proceeds without a completed suit check and a Commander go.**

## 2. Pre-EVA checklist (EVA-PRE)

1. **Suit integrity** — inspect seals, gloves, and helmet for wear or leaks.
2. **O₂ supply** — confirm primary and reserve tanks are full.
3. **Comms** — verify two-way radio with the habitat.
4. **Buddy** — no solo EVAs; every excursion is a pair.
5. **Weather** — check the dust index. Do **not** begin an EVA during an active dust
   storm (dust index above 150).

## 3. Suit leak response (EVA-LEAK)

If a suit leak is detected before egress:
1. **Abort** the EVA for that crew member.
2. **Swap** the faulty seal or suit component.
3. Re-run the pre-EVA suit check before reconsidering.

If a leak is detected **during** an EVA:
1. The affected crew member **returns to the airlock immediately** with their buddy.
2. Switch to reserve O₂ if primary pressure drops.
3. Medical performs a post-EVA check on return.

## 4. Return and post-EVA

- Re-pressurise the airlock fully before removing helmets.
- Log suit hours and any anomalies.
- Report fatigue or discomfort to Medic; minor fatigue after a long surface walk is
  normal, but chest pain or dizziness is not.
